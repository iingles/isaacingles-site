Isaac Ingles
Yeah, me and my friends just bought a Canon 5D Mark II and have been playing around with it; mostly working material and experimenting with different ways to tell stories.

I know that Jesse is still writing stuff; I have been writing on and off as well; but I'm not sure either of us are still on the same page as far as a story is concerned. A lot of it goes back to the fact that I'm simply not a very good writer. Jesse has a lot of potential, though; and I think that he's gotten a lot better from the last stuff he was writing that he told me about.

In my opinion the problem with the story we were working on was that there was really no story. We had zombies, and a superhuman anti-zombie. In most zombie movies the thing that drives the story is survival. When you have a superhuman anti-zombie guy as your main character, you basically rip the guts - survival - out of the genre - he has no reason to be afraid, basically no reason to even care about the zombies - hence no drama, and therefore no story.

My own research has shown me that especially in these type of stories, the weaker the protagonist (to an extent), the better. For example, take a 50 year old woman that has to kill her own sons after they become zombies; and in the end becomes a community leader but carries the psychological scars of having to kill her own children - this leaves a lot of room for character growth and sub-plots.

Another example (though much darker and probably unmarketable) is a group of kids holed up in an elementary school during the zombie holocaust (Lord of the Flies revisited). I've actually written out a couple of sequences from this story; it's totally jacked.

But do you understand what I mean? With zombies it is really difficult to write a story when your main character is basically invulnerable to them. There is nothing to drive the story.

Now, one idea that I had that could make this work is perhaps a post-apocalyptic version where giant mutants, not zombies, are threatening this town; and our main character, although very smart, is not superhuman; the story leads to a climax where the main character has to face off with the mutants in a junkyard; he has to fashion weapons out of old twisted metal and outsmart the mutants by luring them into traps; I've written one scene from this where the guy gets an old car to work and pins a mutant against a wall, then melodramatically gets out of the car and walks up to it, a few words are exchanged, and he slits the creatures throat.

Keep in mind that I'm not really a writer and all this stuff is the opinion of a lighting guy. But let me know what you think anyway.
